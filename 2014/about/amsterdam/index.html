Amsterdam

    About Amsterdam

    World famous and centuries old, but still small enough to easily navigate by foot or bicycle, Amsterdam is one of Europe’s most action-packed capitals, offering everything from high brow culture to low brow entertainment. Whether you’re in the mood for a gourmet meal and a museum visit or a night eating vegan food, smoking, and dancing at one of the city’s many squats-turned-cultural venues, Amsterdam is sure to have something for you.
    On this page you'll find information to help you get the most out of your visit to Amsterdam. Looking for a place to rent a bike, chow down, or get your party on? Below you’ll find a selection of our local favorites. If you’re looking for something specific and you can’t find it here, you can always ask the Windmill crew for their insider tips.

    Tourist Info

    Want to check out the city while you’re in town? In our opinion, no trip to Amsterdam would be complete without seeing one of the city’s world famous museums (especially the Stedelijk Museum, Van Gogh Museum and Rijksmuseum), checking out the old crooked houses and tree-lined canals and monumental churches in the city center (especially the Jordaan neighborhood), chilling in one of the city’s many beautiful parks (the Vondelpark is a classic), and maybe even paying a visit to one of the city’s many coffeeshops.

    Bikes

    If you plan on leaving the fields to explore Amsterdam, we recommend renting a bike to get around. Cycling in Amsterdam is very safe, more convenient than using public transportation, and also not much more expensive, if at all. The metro, trams, and regular buses cost 2,80 for a single ticket and stop running around 12:30AM, after which you can only take a night bus to get back to the fields at a cost of 4,50 per person. On the other hand, renting a bike for 3 days generally costs about 18 euros per person.

    Restaurants

    As a major European capital, Amsterdam is naturally home to restaurants from all of the world’s major cuisines. However, if you want to eat like the Dutch do, we recommend trying out one of the city’s many delicious and affordable Surinamese or Indonesian restaurants, or traditional Dutch cuisine such as pancakes. If you’re in the mood for a late night snack, nothing hits the spot like Belgian fries (‘Vlaamse Frites’ - try the ‘Oorlog’ sauce!), a falafel from MAOZ or a greasy treat pulled out of the wall at one of the city’s many FEBO snack bars. As the Dutch would say: Eet smakelijk! (Bon apetit!)

    Spotted By Locals

    Bars & Clubs

    Amsterdam is home to over 1200 bars and clubs, so there’s a place for everyone to enjoy themselves into the wee hours of the morning.

    De Nieuwe Anita

    Do tell: A very charming little bar in a former squat, decorated like your grandma’s living room and patronized by a hip and international crowd, that hosts interesting entertainment throughout the week.
    Address: Frederik Hendrikstraat 115
    Distance from fields: 6,2 km

    Casablanca

    Do tell: This karaoke bar near Central Station is one of the most infamous spots in Amsterdam, plus it stays open late.
    Address: Zeedijk 26
    Distance from fields: 8,6 km

    Paradiso

    Do tell: This legendary venue in a squatted former church hosts (indie) rock concerts and dance nights seven days a week.
    Address: Weteringschans 6-8
    Distance from fields: 7,9 km

    Pacific Parc

    Do tell: This former factory space now houses a restaurant/cafe by day and bar/club by night known for its eclectic music selection, diverse crowd, and - perhaps most importantly - free entry.
    Address: Polonceaukade 23
    Distance from fields: 6,4 km
