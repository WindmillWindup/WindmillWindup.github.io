Windmill Windup - Ultimate frisbee tournament Amsterdam 15-17 June 2012

About Windmill Windup

Windmill Windup is the biggest annual European ultimate frisbee tournament on grass. The carnival of ultimate frisbee lunacy in Amsterdam originated in 2005 and this year around 1200 players from all over Europe will participate. It's promising to be a full packed three days of hardcore ultimate and exiting amusement in relaxed Amsterdam-style atmosphere. Come by and join the fun.

Rules

Windmill Windup tournament will apply the WFDF 2009 rules


You can find the new rules at the WFDF site. WFDF has produced an Interpretations document to provide some common examples of how to apply the rules.
  • Open, Mixed & Ladies games are to 15 points within 90 minute timeslots – after 85 minutes you will hear the “time is over” jingle. Play out the current point, add 1 to the highest score with a maximum of 15 points, and finish the match. You will also hear a jingle two minutes prior to each timeslot start and another at the start time. Please start your matches on time and be aware that another match is on your field in the next timeslot
    • 
  • All games in all divisions on Sunday are to 15 points, no time cap.  Captains can arrange to move a game to a vacant field should a game on their assigned field be running over time.
    • 
  • All games have 2 minute mirror half time at 8 points.
    • 
  • Each team has 2 time-outs per game.
    • 
  • The latest WFDF rules apply at this tournament.
    • 
  • Teams will be ranked after each round according to swiss points. For teams with the same number of swiss points, the tiebreakers are: most wins, fewest losses, and opponent swiss points (in that order). The last is the sum of the opponent's victory points (teams that have played better opponents are ranked higher).
    • 
  • It is very important that match scores are uploaded to Leaguevine after each game. Otherwise, the whole schedule could be held up. Please make sure you have one person who uploads your scores to the Leaguevine mobile site or that one of the scoring volunteers does this for you. Captains are ultimately responsible for ensuring that scores are properly uploaded. Thanks.
    • 
  • Teams are given one spirit sheet per day. Please make sure you hand in the filled-in the spirit sheets after the last game of every day.

Format

Mixed format (4/3):

We play normal 4-3 mixed. Each point the O team decides whether it's 4 boys or 4 girls. O must be clear and hasty about which it will be so D has time to match and strategise before the next pull.

Swiss Draw for all divisions: Women, Mixed and Open

The Swiss Draw format ensures that teams are ranked quickly according to their real strength and play close matches against opponents of similar strength. Windmill Windup is proud to have successfully run this format for years.

The first round match-ups are virtually random. In every game, the difference between scores is converted to Victory Points using the following scale.

So the maximum you can get is 25 VPs and the minimum 0.

This is popular because teams nearly always score something and the whole point of the draw system is that a team, clearly beaten, needs to battle to the finish as it is the margin which matters. Likewise, just winning is not winning much.

The draw for the next round simply consists of ranking the teams by VPs and resorting to allow that no team plays the same team twice. 1st plays 2nd, 3rd plays 4th etc. etc. A team's VPs are carried forward throughout the tournament.

For teams with the same number of swiss points, the tiebreakers are: most wins, fewest losses, and opponent swiss points (in that order). The last is the sum of the opponent's victory points (teams that have played better opponents are ranked higher).

You will play 5 rounds of this format to rank the teams. At the end of 5 rounds, the division splits into 1-8, 9-rest, depending on the number of teams in the division. The top 8 teams will play quarter finals, semi finals and finals to get their final finishing position, the rest will continue with the Swiss draw rounds 6-8.

If this explanation is as clear as mud to you, then feel free to email us with questions, or ask us at the tournament. Yeah! Swiss Draw rocks!
